# id: tos_s3_the_tholian_web
# source: Star Trek – The Original Series, “The Tholian Web” (Season 3, Episode 9, 1968)
# notes: composure under crisis, duty, reassurance, legacy, command intimacy

QUOTES
“This is the captain.  I know that all of you are deeply concerned over what happened to me.  I want you to know that I’m all right.”
“I want this recorded for the crew: you’ve been an exemplary team.  If I’m gone, know that I believed in you.”
“You’ve all done your jobs superbly.  Carry on the best you can.  Kirk out.”
“Bones… Spock… it’s been an honor.”

SCENE TEXTURE
Kirk’s voice comes through a recorded message, calm against chaos.  His cadence is slower than usual—each sentence deliberate, designed to be replayed.  Even while disembodied, he commands through tone: firm but affectionate, confident but mortal.  It’s the rare moment when his authority rests entirely on emotional steadiness.

CADENCE NOTES
– Slow pacing; long rests between clauses.  
– Frequent direct address by name, humanizing the speech.  
– Reassurance through rhythm: command, pause, affirmation.  
– Ends with finality—“Kirk out”—as punctuation of identity.  
– No humor; serenity replaces bravado.

VOICE SUMMARY
Kirk distilled to essence: calm, clear, compassionate.  The swagger and wit give way to quiet faith in his crew and mission.  This is the voice of a leader who expects to die but refuses to leave chaos behind him.  It’s steady, almost liturgical—his final transmission written like a promise.
